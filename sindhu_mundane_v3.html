how I map to the role

required

Operator excellence. End-to-end ownership in demanding environments.

At Deloitte, owned the full arc from requirements to QA sign-off across 15+ agile sprints for a $204B client. At JHH, ran 20+ stakeholder interviews, analyzed OR data independently, and produced a recommendation with real projected impact. I don't hand things off at the hard parts.

required

Strong analytical skills — comfort with models, metrics, and incomplete information.

At Flowbotic, built a custom Labor Replacement Willingness Index (LRWI) — a weighted scoring model (0.6 × Labor Cost Index + 0.4 × Living Cost Index) to rank 9 European markets for robot deployment priority. Scored each market across 8 variables including AGV market revenue, competitor density, industry mix, and job vacancy rate. France ranked #1 at $58.59M AGV market revenue with the largest 3PL and automotive footprint. Then designed a metrics readiness framework mapping exactly which operational data Flowbotic needed to collect before unlocking each pricing tier — from service bundles to RaaS.

required

Support financial planning. Identify execution gaps and design systems to address them.

At Flowbotic, ran a full pricing audit and identified 10 uncaptured value drivers — including labor reduction, safety improvement, ERP/WMS integration, AI-enabled efficiency, and operational analytics — none of which were being monetized. Recommended a TCO-based sales approach (€/pallet moved) to shift the enterprise conversation from unit price to total cost of ownership. This is the same financial framing that unlocks larger contracts and longer sales cycles.

required

Connective layer across engineering, product, and operations.

At Deloitte: formal bridge between engineering, business, and QA — translated requirements into PRDs with acceptance criteria and held engineering accountable to them. At JHH: coordinated across surgeons, nurses, and administrators simultaneously. At Flowbotic: aligned competitive positioning, software differentiation roadmap, AI strategy, safety framework, and pricing model into one coherent platform strategy.

first 30 days

days 1 – 10

listen and map

  • Shadow the founding team across every recurring meeting
  • Map where decisions get made and where they stall
  • Identify the top 3 things pulling founders from their highest-leverage work
  • Read every internal doc, deck, and customer interview on file

days 11 – 21

identify and structure

  • Surface the 2–3 highest-friction execution gaps slowing the team
  • Draft a lightweight operating rhythm — weekly priorities, async updates
  • Take ownership of one cross-functional initiative end-to-end
  • Build a stakeholder map for key external relationships

days 22 – 30

own and accelerate

  • Present a structured view of execution gaps with proposed fixes
  • Have at least one initiative fully off the founders' plates
  • Establish a repeatable system for tracking cross-team commitments
  • Propose a 60-day scope based on what I've learned

why Mundane specifically

I have worked inside the industrial robotics market. At Flowbotic I scored 9 European markets, mapped MiR, ABB, and 13 other competitors, and identified the strategic gap between hardware-led vendors and what enterprise buyers actually want: integration certainty, TCO visibility, and deployment predictability. Mundane's bet on embodied intelligence and real commercial deployment sits at exactly that gap.

I also understand what slows robot deployments down at the site level — ERP integration complexity, workflow readiness gaps, safety compliance overhead — because I built the frameworks to diagnose and address them. That operational depth, combined with the analytical and cross-functional work I've done, makes me a different kind of CoS candidate for a robotics company.

A seed-stage CoS role is where I want to be. The scope is real, the stakes are real, and the team is small enough that judgment and execution actually compound. I am not looking for a structured internship. I want to help build something.
